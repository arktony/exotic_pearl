Primate safaris

Crafted to give a client an exclusive experience with ape and primates, this safari brings you to the lush green forests of Kibale, Bwindi impenetrable and Mgahinga national park.
Uganda prides of over 50% of the remaining total population of mountain gorillas living in two national parks and that’s Bwindi and Mgahinga then a healthy population of chimpanzees that are widely spread a cross the country. The closest habitat is the Uganda wildlife education center aka zoo in Entebbe then the Ngamba island chimpanzee sanctuary an island that was dedicated to chimpanzees rescued from wildlife trade and some that are orphaned. One can also track chimpanzees in their natural habitats in various national parks and forest reserves like Murchison falls national park in Budongo forest reserve, Bugoma forest reserve, Queen Elizabeth national park in the kyambura gorge, Kalinzu forest reserve that’s found right on the outskirts of queen Elizabeth national park and the motherland of primates aka world’s primate capital i.e. Kibale national park.
To take you on a trip exploring human closest cousins in natural habitats, here our tailored primate safari package

Day 1

We welcome you to the pearl of Africa and our tour guide will meet you at Entebbe International Airport and drive you to your first night destination in Entebbe.
Choice accommodation is ViaVia

Day 2

Early morning drive off to Fort-portal the city of Tooro kingdom where Kibale National Park. This drive lasts for approximately 6hrs with a stop over in Fort for lunch at later proceed to the lodge. A drive from fort to Kibale is 50 minutes and we have selected Kibale Forest Camp to granted you an excellent feel of the wild serenity and ambiance.
Discuss with the guide on time to leave the lodge, briefing time starts at 8Am.
Accommodation; Kibale Forest Camp

Day 3

Chimpanzee Tracking in Kibale National Park
Both AM and PM experiences. We have intentionally offered two tracking experiences for this trip for various reasons.
Playing according to nature rules, in as much as you stand 90% chances to see these creatures having two tracking experiences grants bigger opportunities.
For photographers two tracking experiences gives an opportunity for review and correction plus new composition.
Remember these are not experiences that are had any time one chooses to so why not have them to the fullest when the opportunity comes through.
The AM tracking starts at 8am meaning you will have to be at the briefing point by 7:50am. After tracking the guide will drive you back to the lodge and after lunch you will drive back to VIC (visitor information center) for the PM tracking that starts at 2pm.
Accommodation; Kibale Forest Camp.

Day 4

Transfer to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park
This morning you will drive off from Kibale Forest Camp at 8am and lead to a chosen sector of Bwindi where permits of gorilla tracking will have been secured in advance. A gorilla permit is $800 with effect from 1 st July 2024 in Uganda and habituation is $1500.
We have set this up for clients to make an option on which experience they would find more appealing. The difference in the two is habituation is similar to gorilla tracking but more engaging. Clients get an opportunity to be part of the process of making gorillas accept humans in their territories. Habituation unlike gorilla tracking that is 1 hour with gorilla family is 4 hours of gorilla interaction. Habituation is ideal for photographers and a better experience from our experience.
Accommodation; determined after acquiring the gorilla tracking permits. For the case of habituation that’s only done in Rushaga sector and in this case, we opt for Rushaga Gorilla Safari Lodge

Day 5

Gorilla tracking
Briefing starts at 8am.
We recommend you leave the lodge early enough to be at the VIC in time.
Accommodation is similar to the previous night.

Day 6

Transfer to Kisoro
In Mgahinga national park is where “Gold meets sliver.” This phrase points to the existence of mountain gorillas (silverback) and Golden Monkeys in the same habitat. Mgahinga has one family of mountain gorillas and families of the elusive Golden monkeys. What’s a primate safari without these beautiful rare species!
You will drive from Bwindi heading to Kisoro and this transfer should fall in between 3-4hrs of drive.
Accommodation; Mount Gahinga Safari lodge

Day 7

Tracking Golden Monkeys and Batwa community experience.
The golden monkey tracking experience starts at 8am with the briefing from the Uganda wildlife authority rangers and lasts between 30mins to 3hrs depending on how far the golden monkeys have moved. When all this done then you will return to the lodge for lunch and later in the evening engage in the Batwa experience.
Accommodation; Mount Gahinga Safari Lodge

Day 8

Transfer to Entebbe
This morning you will embark on a journey that winds up the entire experience back to Entebbe. This is a long drive of roughly 8-9hrs however you can opt for a flight to Entebbe that takes approximately 2hrs to your hotel in Entebbe.
Should you opt for a flight we shall arrange a day room for you in Entebbe and transfer you to the airport in check in time for your flight.
